Religion

Jewish, actively practice

What does Judaism mean to me?

I am proud to be Jewish, and my faith is an important part of my life. I actively participate in religious practices, from attending synagogue to celebrating Jewish holidays like Shabbat, Passover, and Hanukkah. For me, my religion is not just a set of beliefs but an outlet to connect with my community, fostering a sense of belonging and shared purpose. Being part of a vibrant Jewish community helps me deepen my connection to my heritage and traditions. I encapsuled these feelings when I had my Bar Mitzvah in 2023.

What's a Bar Mitzvah?

A Bar Mitzvah is a significant milestone in a Jewish boy's life, marking the transition into adulthood at the age of 13. For me, my Bar Mitzvah was a meaningful moment of personal growth, especially since my Torah portion was B'reishit. As I read through it, I found myself deeply connected to its themes, particularly the contrasts of life it presents. The portion reflects the dualities of creation, good and bad, light and dark, which I see mirrored in the challenges and experiences I encounter every day. It was a powerful reminder of the complexities of life and my own role in navigating them.
