Sports

Hockey and Baseball

Overall:

Over the past 7 years, I’ve dedicated myself to hockey, honing my skills in skating, puck handling, and teamwork. Whether on ice or roller, I’ve developed quick thinking, endurance, and communication, while overcoming challenges and building lasting friendships along the way. In baseball, I’ve spent 5 years refining my skills in hitting, fielding, and pitching. The slower, more strategic nature of the game has taught me patience, precision, and focus, helping me stay composed under pressure and adapt to changing situations. Both sports have shaped my athleticism and teamwork, making me a more versatile and resilient player.

Hockey

I've been playing hockey since I was 7 years old. I was always around the sport growing up, and I think that inevitably, the exposure to the game is what cultivated my love for it. It is now my main sport. This is my first year with Lane Tech's combined team, the Chicago North. We ended the regular season with a record of 10-11-1, and we've now fought our way into the semifinals of the playoffs.

Baseball

I started playing baseball when I was 6, and stopped playing around age 12. I stopped playing because hockey was taking up too much of my time and I had started to lose interest in the sport. Dispite stopping, I still made many lasting memories while playing with my teammates and coaches. I played house league baseball at Warren Park from age 6-10, fall ball at Welles Park at age 10, and I played for the Warren Park Warriors travel team from age 10-12.
